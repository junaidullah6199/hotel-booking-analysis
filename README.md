# Hotel Booking Cancellation Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes hotel booking data (2015–2017) to understand factors influencing reservation cancellations and their impact on revenue and occupancy.

The objective was to identify patterns in cancellation behavior and provide business recommendations to reduce revenue loss.

---

## Business Problem
City Hotel and Resort Hotel experienced high cancellation rates, leading to reduced revenue and inefficient room utilization.

---

## Dataset
- 119,390 booking records
- Time period: 2015–2017
- Includes:
  - Reservation Status
  - Hotel Type
  - Average Daily Rate (ADR)
  - Booking Channel
  - Country
  - Arrival Month
  - Customer Type

---

## Tools Used
- Python
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Jupyter Notebook

---

## Key Findings

- 37% of bookings were canceled.
- Higher Average Daily Rate (ADR) is associated with increased cancellation rates.
- January had the highest number of cancellations.
- August had the highest total reservations.
- 46% of bookings came from Online Travel Agencies.
- Portugal recorded the highest cancellation volume.
- Resort hotels showed a higher cancellation ratio compared to city hotels.

---

## Business Recommendations

- Implement dynamic pricing strategies during high-risk months.
- Offer targeted discounts during January to reduce cancellations.
- Improve service quality in high-cancellation regions.
- Optimize weekend and holiday pricing for resort hotels.
- Diversify booking channels to reduce dependency on online agencies.
